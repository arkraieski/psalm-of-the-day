---
number: 56
title: "For the Chief Musician. To the tune of “Silent Dove in Distant Lands.” A poem by David, when the Philistines seized him in Gath."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Be merciful to me, God, for man wants to swallow me up.
All day long, he attacks and oppresses me.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> My enemies want to swallow me up all day long,
for they are many who fight proudly against me.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> When I am afraid,
I will put my trust in you.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> In God, I praise his word.
In God, I put my trust.
I will not be afraid.
What can flesh do to me?

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> All day long they twist my words.
All their thoughts are against me for evil.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> They conspire and lurk,
watching my steps.
They are eager to take my life.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Shall they escape by iniquity?
In anger cast down the peoples, God.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> You count my wanderings.
You put my tears into your container.
Aren’t they in your book?

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Then my enemies shall turn back in the day that I call.
I know this: that God is for me.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> In God, I will praise his word.
In Yahweh, I will praise his word.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> I have put my trust in God.
I will not be afraid.
What can man do to me?

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Your vows are on me, God.
I will give thank offerings to you.

<sup class="verse-num">13</sup> For you have delivered my soul from death,
and prevented my feet from falling,
that I may walk before God in the light of the living.
