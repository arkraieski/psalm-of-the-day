---
number: 52
title: "For the Chief Musician. A contemplation by David, when Doeg the Edomite came and told Saul, “David has come to Ahimelech’s house.”"
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Why do you boast of mischief, mighty man?
God’s loving kindness endures continually.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Your tongue plots destruction,
like a sharp razor, working deceitfully.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> You love evil more than good,
lying rather than speaking the truth.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> You love all devouring words,
you deceitful tongue.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> God will likewise destroy you forever.
He will take you up, and pluck you out of your tent,
and root you out of the land of the living.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The righteous also will see it, and fear,
and laugh at him, saying,

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> “Behold, this is the man who didn’t make God his strength,
but trusted in the abundance of his riches,
and strengthened himself in his wickedness.”

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> But as for me, I am like a green olive tree in God’s house.
I trust in God’s loving kindness forever and ever.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> I will give you thanks forever, because you have done it.
I will hope in your name, for it is good,
in the presence of your saints.
