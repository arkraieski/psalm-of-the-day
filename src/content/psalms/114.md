<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> When Israel went out of Egypt,
the house of Jacob from a people of foreign language,

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Judah became his sanctuary,
Israel his dominion.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The sea saw it, and fled.
The Jordan was driven back.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> The mountains skipped like rams,
the little hills like lambs.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> What was it, you sea, that you fled?
You Jordan, that you turned back?

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> You mountains, that you skipped like rams;
you little hills, like lambs?

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Tremble, you earth, at the presence of the Lord,
at the presence of the God of Jacob,

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> who turned the rock into a pool of water,
the flint into a spring of waters.
