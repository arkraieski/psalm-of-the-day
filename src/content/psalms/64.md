---
number: 64
title: "For the Chief Musician. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Hear my voice, God, in my complaint.
Preserve my life from fear of the enemy.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Hide me from the conspiracy of the wicked,
from the noisy crowd of the ones doing evil;

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> who sharpen their tongue like a sword,
and aim their arrows, deadly words,

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> to shoot innocent men from ambushes.
They shoot at him suddenly and fearlessly.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They encourage themselves in evil plans.
They talk about laying snares secretly.
They say, “Who will see them?”

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> They plot injustice, saying, “We have made a perfect plan!”
Surely man’s mind and heart are cunning.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> But God will shoot at them.
They will be suddenly struck down with an arrow.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Their own tongues shall ruin them.
All who see them will shake their heads.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> All mankind shall be afraid.
They shall declare the work of God,
and shall wisely ponder what he has done.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The righteous shall be glad in Yahweh,
and shall take refuge in him.
All the upright in heart shall praise him!
