<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down.
Yes, we wept, when we remembered Zion.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> On the willows in that land,
we hung up our harps.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> For there, those who led us captive asked us for songs.
Those who tormented us demanded songs of joy:
“Sing us one of the songs of Zion!”

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> How can we sing Yahweh’s song in a foreign land?

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> If I forget you, Jerusalem,
let my right hand forget its skill.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Let my tongue stick to the roof of my mouth if I don’t remember you,
if I don’t prefer Jerusalem above my chief joy.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Remember, Yahweh, against the children of Edom in the day of Jerusalem,
who said, “Raze it!
Raze it even to its foundation!”

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Daughter of Babylon, doomed to destruction,
he will be happy who repays you,
as you have done to us.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Happy shall he be,
who takes and dashes your little ones against the rock.
