---
number: 6
title: "For the Chief Musician; on stringed instruments, upon the eight-stringed lyre. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Yahweh, don’t rebuke me in your anger,
neither discipline me in your wrath.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Have mercy on me, Yahweh, for I am faint.
Yahweh, heal me, for my bones are troubled.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> My soul is also in great anguish.
But you, Yahweh—how long?

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Return, Yahweh. Deliver my soul,
and save me for your loving kindness’ sake.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> For in death there is no memory of you.
In Sheol, who shall give you thanks?

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> I am weary with my groaning.
Every night I flood my bed.
I drench my couch with my tears.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> My eye wastes away because of grief.
It grows old because of all my adversaries.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Depart from me, all you workers of iniquity,
for Yahweh has heard the voice of my weeping.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Yahweh has heard my supplication.
Yahweh accepts my prayer.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> May all my enemies be ashamed and dismayed.
They shall turn back, they shall be disgraced suddenly.
