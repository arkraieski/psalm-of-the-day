---
number: 12
title: "For the Chief Musician; upon an eight-stringed lyre. A Psalm of David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Help, Yahweh; for the godly man ceases.
For the faithful fail from among the children of men.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Everyone lies to his neighbor.
They speak with flattering lips, and with a double heart.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> May Yahweh cut off all flattering lips,
and the tongue that boasts,

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> who have said, “With our tongue we will prevail.
Our lips are our own.
Who is lord over us?”

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> “Because of the oppression of the weak and because of the groaning of the needy,
I will now arise,” says Yahweh;
“I will set him in safety from those who malign him.”

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Yahweh’s words are flawless words,
as silver refined in a clay furnace, purified seven times.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> You will keep them, Yahweh.
You will preserve them from this generation forever.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> The wicked walk on every side,
when what is vile is exalted among the sons of men.
