---
number: 41
title: "For the Chief Musician. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Blessed is he who considers the poor.
Yahweh will deliver him in the day of evil.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Yahweh will preserve him, and keep him alive.
He shall be blessed on the earth,
and he will not surrender him to the will of his enemies.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Yahweh will sustain him on his sickbed,
and restore him from his bed of illness.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> I said, “Yahweh, have mercy on me!
Heal me, for I have sinned against you.”

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> My enemies speak evil against me:
“When will he die, and his name perish?”

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> If he comes to see me, he speaks falsehood.
His heart gathers iniquity to itself.
When he goes abroad, he tells it.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> All who hate me whisper together against me.
They imagine the worst for me.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> “An evil disease”, they say, “has afflicted him.
Now that he lies he shall rise up no more.”

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Yes, my own familiar friend, in whom I trusted,
who ate bread with me,
has lifted up his heel against me.


<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> But you, Yahweh, have mercy on me, and raise me up,
that I may repay them.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> By this I know that you delight in me,
because my enemy doesn’t triumph over me.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> As for me, you uphold me in my integrity,
and set me in your presence forever.


<sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Blessed be Yahweh, the God of Israel,
from everlasting and to everlasting!
Amen and amen.
