---
number: 62
title: "For the Chief Musician. To Jeduthun. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> My soul rests in God alone.
My salvation is from him.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> He alone is my rock, my salvation, and my fortress.
I will never be greatly shaken.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> How long will you assault a man?
Would all of you throw him down,
like a leaning wall, like a tottering fence?

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> They fully intend to throw him down from his lofty place.
They delight in lies.
They bless with their mouth, but they curse inwardly.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> My soul, wait in silence for God alone,
for my expectation is from him.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> He alone is my rock and my salvation, my fortress.
I will not be shaken.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> My salvation and my honor is with God.
The rock of my strength, and my refuge, is in God.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Trust in him at all times, you people.
Pour out your heart before him.
God is a refuge for us.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Surely men of low degree are just a breath,
and men of high degree are a lie.
In the balances they will go up.
They are together lighter than a breath.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Don’t trust in oppression.
Don’t become vain in robbery.
If riches increase,
don’t set your heart on them.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> God has spoken once;
twice I have heard this,
that power belongs to God.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Also to you, Lord, belongs loving kindness,
for you reward every man according to his work.
