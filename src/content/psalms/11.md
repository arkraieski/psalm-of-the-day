---
number: 11
title: "For the Chief Musician. By David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> In Yahweh, I take refuge.
How can you say to my soul, “Flee as a bird to your mountain”?

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> For, behold, the wicked bend their bows.
They set their arrows on the strings,
that they may shoot in darkness at the upright in heart.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> If the foundations are destroyed,
what can the righteous do?

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Yahweh is in his holy temple.
Yahweh is on his throne in heaven.
His eyes observe.
His eyes examine the children of men.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Yahweh examines the righteous,
but his soul hates the wicked and him who loves violence.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> On the wicked he will rain blazing coals;
fire, sulfur, and scorching wind shall be the portion of their cup.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> For Yahweh is righteous.
He loves righteousness.
The upright shall see his face.
