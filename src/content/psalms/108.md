---
number: 108
title: "A Song. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> My heart is steadfast, God.
I will sing and I will make music with my soul.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Wake up, harp and lyre!
I will wake up the dawn.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> I will give thanks to you, Yahweh, among the nations.
I will sing praises to you among the peoples.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> For your loving kindness is great above the heavens.
Your faithfulness reaches to the skies.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Be exalted, God, above the heavens!
Let your glory be over all the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> That your beloved may be delivered,
save with your right hand, and answer us.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> God has spoken from his sanctuary: “In triumph,
I will divide Shechem, and measure out the valley of Succoth.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Gilead is mine. Manasseh is mine.
Ephraim also is my helmet.
Judah is my scepter.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Moab is my wash pot.
I will toss my sandal on Edom.
I will shout over Philistia.”

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Who will bring me into the fortified city?
Who has led me to Edom?

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Haven’t you rejected us, God?
You don’t go out, God, with our armies.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Give us help against the enemy,
for the help of man is vain.

<sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Through God, we will do valiantly.
For it is he who will tread down our enemies.
