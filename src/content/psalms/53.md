---
number: 53
title: "For the Chief Musician. To the tune of “Mahalath.” A contemplation by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The fool has said in his heart, “There is no God.”
They are corrupt, and have done abominable iniquity.
There is no one who does good.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> God looks down from heaven on the children of men,
to see if there are any who understood,
who seek after God.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Every one of them has gone back.
They have become filthy together.
There is no one who does good, no, not one.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Have the workers of iniquity no knowledge,
who eat up my people as they eat bread,
and don’t call on God?

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> There they were in great fear, where no fear was,
for God has scattered the bones of him who encamps against you.
You have put them to shame,
because God has rejected them.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Oh that the salvation of Israel would come out of Zion!
When God brings back his people from captivity,
then Jacob shall rejoice,
and Israel shall be glad.
