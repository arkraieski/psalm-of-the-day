---
number: 58
title: "For the Chief Musician. To the tune of “Do Not Destroy.” A poem by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Do you indeed speak righteousness, silent ones?
Do you judge blamelessly, you sons of men?

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> No, in your heart you plot injustice.
You measure out the violence of your hands in the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The wicked go astray from the womb.
They are wayward as soon as they are born, speaking lies.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Their poison is like the poison of a snake,
like a deaf cobra that stops its ear,

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> which doesn’t listen to the voice of charmers,
no matter how skillful the charmer may be.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Break their teeth, God, in their mouth.
Break out the great teeth of the young lions, Yahweh.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Let them vanish like water that flows away.
When they draw the bow, let their arrows be made blunt.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Let them be like a snail which melts and passes away,
like the stillborn child, who has not seen the sun.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Before your pots can feel the heat of the thorns,
he will sweep away the green and the burning alike.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The righteous shall rejoice when he sees the vengeance.
He shall wash his feet in the blood of the wicked,

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> so that men shall say, “Most certainly there is a reward for the righteous.
Most certainly there is a God who judges the earth.”
