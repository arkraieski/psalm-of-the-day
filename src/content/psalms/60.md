---
number: 60
title: "For the Chief Musician. To the tune of “The Lily of the Covenant.” A teaching poem by David, when he fought with Aram Naharaim and with Aram Zobah, and Joab returned, and killed twelve thousand of Edom in the Valley of Salt."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> God, you have rejected us.
You have broken us down.
You have been angry.
Restore us, again.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> You have made the land tremble.
You have torn it.
Mend its fractures,
for it quakes.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> You have shown your people hard things.
You have made us drink the wine that makes us stagger.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> You have given a banner to those who fear you,
that it may be displayed because of the truth.


<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> So that your beloved may be delivered,
save with your right hand, and answer us.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> God has spoken from his sanctuary:
“I will triumph.
I will divide Shechem,
and measure out the valley of Succoth.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Gilead is mine, and Manasseh is mine.
Ephraim also is the defense of my head.
Judah is my scepter.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Moab is my wash basin.
I will throw my sandal on Edom.
I shout in triumph over Philistia.”


<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Who will bring me into the strong city?
Who has led me to Edom?

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Haven’t you, God, rejected us?
You don’t go out with our armies, God.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Give us help against the adversary,
for the help of man is vain.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Through God we will do valiantly,
for it is he who will tread down our adversaries.
