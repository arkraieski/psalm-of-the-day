---
number: 4
title: "For the Chief Musician; on stringed instruments. A Psalm by David."
testament: "OT"
---

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Answer me when I call, God of my righteousness.
Give me relief from my distress.
Have mercy on me, and hear my prayer.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> You sons of men, how long shall my glory be turned into dishonor?
Will you love vanity and seek after falsehood?

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> But know that Yahweh has set apart for himself him who is godly:
Yahweh will hear when I call to him.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Stand in awe, and don’t sin.
Search your own heart on your bed, and be still.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Offer the sacrifices of righteousness.
Put your trust in Yahweh.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Many say, “Who will show us any good?”
Yahweh, let the light of your face shine on us.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> You have put gladness in my heart,
more than when their grain and their new wine are increased.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> In peace I will both lay myself down and sleep,
for you, Yahweh alone, make me live in safety.
