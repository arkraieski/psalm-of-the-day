<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> How amiable <em>are</em> thy tabernacles, O LORD of hosts!

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> My soul longeth, yea, even fainteth for the courts of the LORD: my heart and my flesh crieth out for the living God.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Yea, the sparrow hath found an house, and the swallow a nest for herself, where she may lay her young, <em>even</em> thine altars, O LORD of hosts, my King, and my God.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Blessed <em>are</em> they that dwell in thy house: they will be still praising thee. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Blessed <em>is</em> the man whose strength <em>is</em> in thee; in whose heart <em>are</em> the ways <em>of them</em>.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>Who</em> passing through the valley of Baca make it a well; the rain also filleth the pools.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> They go from strength to strength, <em>every one of them</em> in Zion appeareth before God.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> O LORD God of hosts, hear my prayer: give ear, O God of Jacob. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Behold, O God our shield, and look upon the face of thine anointed.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> For a day in thy courts <em>is</em> better than a thousand. I had rather be a doorkeeper in the house of my God, than to dwell in the tents of wickedness.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> For the LORD God <em>is</em> a sun and shield: the LORD will give grace and glory: no good <em>thing</em> will he withhold from them that walk uprightly.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> O LORD of hosts, blessed <em>is</em> the man that trusteth in thee.
