<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Praise ye the LORD. I will praise the LORD with <em>my</em> whole heart, in the assembly of the upright, and <em>in</em> the congregation.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> The works of the LORD <em>are</em> great, sought out of all them that have pleasure therein.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> His work <em>is</em> honourable and glorious: and his righteousness endureth for ever.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> He hath made his wonderful works to be remembered: the LORD <em>is</em> gracious and full of compassion.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> He hath given meat unto them that fear him: he will ever be mindful of his covenant.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> He hath shewed his people the power of his works, that he may give them the heritage of the heathen.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> The works of his hands <em>are</em> verity and judgment; all his commandments <em>are</em> sure.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> They stand fast for ever and ever, <em>and are</em> done in truth and uprightness.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> He sent redemption unto his people: he hath commanded his covenant for ever: holy and reverend <em>is</em> his name.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The fear of the LORD <em>is</em> the beginning of wisdom: a good understanding have all they that do <em>his commandments</em>: his praise endureth for ever.
