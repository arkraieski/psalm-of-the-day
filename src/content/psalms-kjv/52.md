testament: "OT"

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Why boastest thou thyself in mischief, O mighty man? the goodness of God <em>endureth</em> continually.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Thy tongue deviseth mischiefs; like a sharp razor, working deceitfully.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Thou lovest evil more than good; <em>and</em> lying rather than to speak righteousness. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Thou lovest all devouring words, O <em>thou</em> deceitful tongue.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> God shall likewise destroy thee for ever, he shall take thee away, and pluck thee out of <em>thy</em> dwelling place, and root thee out of the land of the living. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The righteous also shall see, and fear, and shall laugh at him:

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Lo, <em>this is</em> the man <em>that</em> made not God his strength; but trusted in the abundance of his riches, <em>and</em> strengthened himself in his wickedness.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> But I <em>am</em> like a green olive tree in the house of God: I trust in the mercy of God for ever and ever.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> I will praise thee for ever, because thou hast done <em>it</em>: and I will wait on thy name; for <em>it is</em> good before thy saints.
