<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Why do the heathen rage, and the people imagine a vain thing?

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> The kings of the earth set themselves, and the rulers take counsel together, against the LORD, and against his anointed, <em>saying</em>,

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Let us break their bands asunder, and cast away their cords from us.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> He that sitteth in the heavens shall laugh: the Lord shall have them in derision.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Then shall he speak unto them in his wrath, and vex them in his sore displeasure.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Yet have I set my king upon my holy hill of Zion.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> I will declare the decree: the LORD hath said unto me, Thou <em>art</em> my Son; this day have I begotten thee.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Ask of me, and I shall give <em>thee</em> the heathen <em>for</em> thine inheritance, and the uttermost parts of the earth <em>for</em> thy possession.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Thou shalt break them with a rod of iron; thou shalt dash them in pieces like a potter's vessel.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Be wise now therefore, O ye kings: be instructed, ye judges of the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Serve the LORD with fear, and rejoice with trembling.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Kiss the Son, lest he be angry, and ye perish <em>from</em> the way, when his wrath is kindled but a little. Blessed <em>are</em> all they that put their trust in him.
