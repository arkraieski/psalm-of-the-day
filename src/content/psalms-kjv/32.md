<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Blessed <em>is he whose</em> transgression <em>is</em> forgiven, <em>whose</em> sin <em>is</em> covered.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Blessed <em>is</em> the man unto whom the LORD imputeth not iniquity, and in whose spirit <em>there is</em> no guile.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> When I kept silence, my bones waxed old through my roaring all the day long.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> For day and night thy hand was heavy upon me: my moisture is turned into the drought of summer. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> I acknowledged my sin unto thee, and mine iniquity have I not hid. I said, I will confess my transgressions unto the LORD; and thou forgavest the iniquity of my sin. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> For this shall every one that is godly pray unto thee in a time when thou mayest be found: surely in the floods of great waters they shall not come nigh unto him.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Thou <em>art</em> my hiding place; thou shalt preserve me from trouble; thou shalt compass me about with songs of deliverance. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I will instruct thee and teach thee in the way which thou shalt go: I will guide thee with mine eye.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Be ye not as the horse, <em>or</em> as the mule, <em>which</em> have no understanding: whose mouth must be held in with bit and bridle, lest they come near unto thee.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Many sorrows <em>shall be</em> to the wicked: but he that trusteth in the LORD, mercy shall compass him about.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Be glad in the LORD, and rejoice, ye righteous: and shout for joy, all <em>ye that are</em> upright in heart.
