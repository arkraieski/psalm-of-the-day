<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> O God, thou hast cast us off, thou hast scattered us, thou hast been displeased; O turn thyself to us again.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Thou hast made the earth to tremble; thou hast broken it: heal the breaches thereof; for it shaketh.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Thou hast shewed thy people hard things: thou hast made us to drink the wine of astonishment.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Thou hast given a banner to them that fear thee, that it may be displayed because of the truth. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> That thy beloved may be delivered; save <em>with</em> thy right hand, and hear me.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> God hath spoken in his holiness; I will rejoice, I will divide Shechem, and mete out the valley of Succoth.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Gilead <em>is</em> mine, and Manasseh <em>is</em> mine; Ephraim also <em>is</em> the strength of mine head; Judah <em>is</em> my lawgiver;

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Moab <em>is</em> my washpot; over Edom will I cast out my shoe: Philistia, triumph thou because of me.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Who will bring me <em>into</em> the strong city? who will lead me into Edom?

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> <em>Wilt</em> not thou, O God, <em>which</em> hadst cast us off? and <em>thou</em>, O God, <em>which</em> didst not go out with our armies?

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Give us help from trouble: for vain <em>is</em> the help of man.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Through God we shall do valiantly: for he <em>it is that</em> shall tread down our enemies.
