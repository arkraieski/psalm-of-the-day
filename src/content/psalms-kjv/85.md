<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> LORD, thou hast been favourable unto thy land: thou hast brought back the captivity of Jacob.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Thou hast forgiven the iniquity of thy people, thou hast covered all their sin. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Thou hast taken away all thy wrath: thou hast turned <em>thyself</em> from the fierceness of thine anger.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Turn us, O God of our salvation, and cause thine anger toward us to cease.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Wilt thou be angry with us for ever? wilt thou draw out thine anger to all generations?

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Wilt thou not revive us again: that thy people may rejoice in thee?

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Shew us thy mercy, O LORD, and grant us thy salvation.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I will hear what God the LORD will speak: for he will speak peace unto his people, and to his saints: but let them not turn again to folly.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Surely his salvation <em>is</em> nigh them that fear him; that glory may dwell in our land.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Mercy and truth are met together; righteousness and peace have kissed <em>each other</em>.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Truth shall spring out of the earth; and righteousness shall look down from heaven.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Yea, the LORD shall give <em>that which is</em> good; and our land shall yield her increase.

<sup class="verse-num">13</sup> Righteousness shall go before him; and shall set <em>us</em> in the way of his steps.
