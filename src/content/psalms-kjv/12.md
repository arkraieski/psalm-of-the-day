<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Help, LORD; for the godly man ceaseth; for the faithful fail from among the children of men.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> They speak vanity every one with his neighbour: <em>with</em> flattering lips <em>and</em> with a double heart do they speak.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The LORD shall cut off all flattering lips, <em>and</em> the tongue that speaketh proud things:

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Who have said, With our tongue will we prevail; our lips <em>are</em> our own: who <em>is</em> lord over us?

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> For the oppression of the poor, for the sighing of the needy, now will I arise, saith the LORD; I will set <em>him</em> in safety <em>from him that</em> puffeth at him.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The words of the LORD <em>are</em> pure words: <em>as</em> silver tried in a furnace of earth, purified seven times.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Thou shalt keep them, O LORD, thou shalt preserve them from this generation for ever.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> The wicked walk on every side, when the vilest men are exalted.
