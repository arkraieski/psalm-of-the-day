<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Hear me when I call, O God of my righteousness: thou hast enlarged me <em>when I was</em> in distress; have mercy upon me, and hear my prayer.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> O ye sons of men, how long <em>will ye turn</em> my glory into shame? <em>how long</em> will ye love vanity, <em>and</em> seek after leasing? Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> But know that the LORD hath set apart him that is godly for himself: the LORD will hear when I call unto him.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Stand in awe, and sin not: commune with your own heart upon your bed, and be still. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Offer the sacrifices of righteousness, and put your trust in the LORD.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> <em>There be</em> many that say, Who will shew us <em>any</em> good? LORD, lift thou up the light of thy countenance upon us.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Thou hast put gladness in my heart, more than in the time <em>that</em> their corn and their wine increased.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> I will both lay me down in peace, and sleep: for thou, LORD, only makest me dwell in safety.
