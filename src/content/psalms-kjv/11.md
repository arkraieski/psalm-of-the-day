<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> In the LORD put I my trust: how say ye to my soul, Flee <em>as</em> a bird to your mountain?

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> For, lo, the wicked bend <em>their</em> bow, they make ready their arrow upon the string, that they may privily shoot at the upright in heart.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> If the foundations be destroyed, what can the righteous do?

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> The LORD <em>is</em> in his holy temple, the LORD'S throne <em>is</em> in heaven: his eyes behold, his eyelids try, the children of men.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The LORD trieth the righteous: but the wicked and him that loveth violence his soul hateth.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Upon the wicked he shall rain snares, fire and brimstone, and an horrible tempest: <em>this shall be</em> the portion of their cup.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> For the righteous LORD loveth righteousness; his countenance doth behold the upright.
