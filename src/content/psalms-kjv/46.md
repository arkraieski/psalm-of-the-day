<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> God <em>is</em> our refuge and strength, a very present help in trouble.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Therefore will not we fear, though the earth be removed, and though the mountains be carried into the midst of the sea;

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> <em>Though</em> the waters thereof roar <em>and</em> be troubled, <em>though</em> the mountains shake with the swelling thereof. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> <em>There is</em> a river, the streams whereof shall make glad the city of God, the holy <em>place</em> of the tabernacles of the most High.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> God <em>is</em> in the midst of her; she shall not be moved: God shall help her, <em>and that</em> right early.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> The heathen raged, the kingdoms were moved: he uttered his voice, the earth melted.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> The LORD of hosts <em>is</em> with us; the God of Jacob <em>is</em> our refuge. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Come, behold the works of the LORD, what desolations he hath made in the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> He maketh wars to cease unto the end of the earth; he breaketh the bow, and cutteth the spear in sunder; he burneth the chariot in the fire.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Be still, and know that I <em>am</em> God: I will be exalted among the heathen, I will be exalted in the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> The LORD of hosts <em>is</em> with us; the God of Jacob <em>is</em> our refuge. Selah.
