<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The earth <em>is</em> the LORD'S, and the fulness thereof; the world, and they that dwell therein.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> For he hath founded it upon the seas, and established it upon the floods.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Who shall ascend into the hill of the LORD? or who shall stand in his holy place?

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> He that hath clean hands, and a pure heart; who hath not lifted up his soul unto vanity, nor sworn deceitfully.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> He shall receive the blessing from the LORD, and righteousness from the God of his salvation.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> This <em>is</em> the generation of them that seek him, that seek thy face, O Jacob. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Lift up your heads, O ye gates; and be ye lift up, ye everlasting doors; and the King of glory shall come in.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Who <em>is</em> this King of glory? The LORD strong and mighty, the LORD mighty in battle.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Lift up your heads, O ye gates; even lift <em>them</em> up, ye everlasting doors; and the King of glory shall come in.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Who is this King of glory? The LORD of hosts, he <em>is</em> the King of glory. Selah.
