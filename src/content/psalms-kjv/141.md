<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> LORD, I cry unto thee: make haste unto me; give ear unto my voice, when I cry unto thee.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Let my prayer be set forth before thee <em>as</em> incense; <em>and</em> the lifting up of my hands <em>as</em> the evening sacrifice.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Set a watch, O LORD, before my mouth; keep the door of my lips.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Incline not my heart to <em>any</em> evil thing, to practise wicked works with men that work iniquity: and let me not eat of their dainties.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Let the righteous smite me; <em>it shall be</em> a kindness: and let him reprove me; <em>it shall be</em> an excellent oil, <em>which</em> shall not break my head: for yet my prayer also <em>shall be</em> in their calamities.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> When their judges are overthrown in stony places, they shall hear my words; for they are sweet.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Our bones are scattered at the grave's mouth, as when one cutteth and cleaveth <em>wood</em> upon the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> But mine eyes <em>are</em> unto thee, O GOD the Lord: in thee is my trust; leave not my soul destitute.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Keep me from the snares <em>which</em> they have laid for me, and the gins of the workers of iniquity.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Let the wicked fall into their own nets, whilst that I withal escape.
