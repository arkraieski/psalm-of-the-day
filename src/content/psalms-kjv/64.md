<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Hear my voice, O God, in my prayer: preserve my life from fear of the enemy.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Hide me from the secret counsel of the wicked; from the insurrection of the workers of iniquity:

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Who whet their tongue like a sword, <em>and</em> bend <em>their bows to shoot</em> their arrows, <em>even</em> bitter words:

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> That they may shoot in secret at the perfect: suddenly do they shoot at him, and fear not.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> They encourage themselves <em>in</em> an evil matter: they commune of laying snares privily; they say, Who shall see them?

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> They search out iniquities; they accomplish a diligent search: both the inward <em>thought</em> of every one <em>of them</em>, and the heart, <em>is</em> deep.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> But God shall shoot at them <em>with</em> an arrow; suddenly shall they be wounded.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> So they shall make their own tongue to fall upon themselves: all that see them shall flee away.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> And all men shall fear, and shall declare the work of God; for they shall wisely consider of his doing.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> The righteous shall be glad in the LORD, and shall trust in him; and all the upright in heart shall glory.
