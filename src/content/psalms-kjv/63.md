testament: "OT"

<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> O God, thou <em>art</em> my God; early will I seek thee: my soul thirsteth for thee, my flesh longeth for thee in a dry and thirsty land, where no water is;

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> To see thy power and thy glory, so <em>as</em> I have seen thee in the sanctuary.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> Because thy lovingkindness <em>is</em> better than life, my lips shall praise thee.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Thus will I bless thee while I live: I will lift up my hands in thy name.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> My soul shall be satisfied as <em>with</em> marrow and fatness; and my mouth shall praise <em>thee</em> with joyful lips:

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> When I remember thee upon my bed, <em>and</em> meditate on thee in the <em>night</em> watches.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Because thou hast been my help, therefore in the shadow of thy wings will I rejoice.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> My soul followeth hard after thee: thy right hand upholdeth me.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> But those <em>that</em> seek my soul, to destroy <em>it</em>, shall go into the lower parts of the earth.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> They shall fall by the sword: they shall be a portion for foxes.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> But the king shall rejoice in God; every one that sweareth by him shall glory: but the mouth of them that speak lies shall be stopped.
