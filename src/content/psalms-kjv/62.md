<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> Truly my soul waiteth upon God: from him <em>cometh</em> my salvation.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> He only <em>is</em> my rock and my salvation; <em>he is</em> my defence; I shall not be greatly moved.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> How long will ye imagine mischief against a man? ye shall be slain all of you: as a bowing wall <em>shall ye be, and as</em> a tottering fence.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> They only consult to cast <em>him</em> down from his excellency: they delight in lies: they bless with their mouth, but they curse inwardly. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> My soul, wait thou only upon God; for my expectation <em>is</em> from him.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> He only <em>is</em> my rock and my salvation: <em>he is</em> my defence; I shall not be moved.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> In God <em>is</em> my salvation and my glory: the rock of my strength, <em>and</em> my refuge, <em>is</em> in God.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Trust in him at all times; <em>ye</em> people, pour out your heart before him: God <em>is</em> a refuge for us. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Surely men of low degree <em>are</em> vanity, <em>and</em> men of high degree <em>are</em> a lie: to be laid in the balance, they <em>are</em> altogether <em>lighter</em> than vanity.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Trust not in oppression, and become not vain in robbery: if riches increase, set not your heart <em>upon them</em>.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> God hath spoken once; twice have I heard this; that power <em>belongeth</em> unto God.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> Also unto thee, O Lord, <em>belongeth</em> mercy: for thou renderest to every man according to his work.
