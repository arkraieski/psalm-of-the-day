<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> When Israel went out of Egypt, the house of Jacob from a people of strange language;

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Judah was his sanctuary, <em>and</em> Israel his dominion.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The sea saw <em>it</em>, and fled: Jordan was driven back.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> The mountains skipped like rams, <em>and</em> the little hills like lambs.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> What <em>ailed</em> thee, O thou sea, that thou fleddest? thou Jordan, <em>that</em> thou wast driven back?

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Ye mountains, <em>that</em> ye skipped like rams; <em>and</em> ye little hills, like lambs?

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Tremble, thou earth, at the presence of the Lord, at the presence of the God of Jacob;

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Which turned the rock <em>into</em> a standing water, the flint into a fountain of waters.
