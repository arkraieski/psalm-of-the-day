<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> The transgression of the wicked saith within my heart, <em>that there is</em> no fear of God before his eyes.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> For he flattereth himself in his own eyes, until his iniquity be found to be hateful.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> The words of his mouth <em>are</em> iniquity and deceit: he hath left off to be wise, <em>and</em> to do good.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> He deviseth mischief upon his bed; he setteth himself in a way <em>that is</em> not good; he abhorreth not evil.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> Thy mercy, O LORD, <em>is</em> in the heavens; <em>and</em> thy faithfulness <em>reacheth</em> unto the clouds.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> Thy righteousness <em>is</em> like the great mountains; thy judgments <em>are</em> a great deep: O LORD, thou preservest man and beast.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> How excellent <em>is</em> thy lovingkindness, O God! therefore the children of men put their trust under the shadow of thy wings.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> They shall be abundantly satisfied with the fatness of thy house; and thou shalt make them drink of the river of thy pleasures.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> For with thee <em>is</em> the fountain of life: in thy light shall we see light.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> O continue thy lovingkindness unto them that know thee; and thy righteousness to the upright in heart.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Let not the foot of pride come against me, and let not the hand of the wicked remove me.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> There are the workers of iniquity fallen: they are cast down, and shall not be able to rise.
