<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> In Judah <em>is</em> God known: his name <em>is</em> great in Israel.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> In Salem also is his tabernacle, and his dwelling place in Zion.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> There brake he the arrows of the bow, the shield, and the sword, and the battle. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> Thou <em>art</em> more glorious <em>and</em> excellent than the mountains of prey.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The stouthearted are spoiled, they have slept their sleep: and none of the men of might have found their hands.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> At thy rebuke, O God of Jacob, both the chariot and horse are cast into a dead sleep.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Thou, <em>even</em> thou, <em>art</em> to be feared: and who may stand in thy sight when once thou art angry?

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Thou didst cause judgment to be heard from heaven; the earth feared, and was still,

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> When God arose to judgment, to save all the meek of the earth. Selah.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Surely the wrath of man shall praise thee: the remainder of wrath shalt thou restrain.

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Vow, and pay unto the LORD your God: let all that be round about him bring presents unto him that ought to be feared.

<sup class="verse-num">12</sup> He shall cut off the spirit of princes: <em>he is</em> terrible to the kings of the earth.
