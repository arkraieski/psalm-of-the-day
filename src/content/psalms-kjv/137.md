<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> By the rivers of Babylon, there we sat down, yea, we wept, when we remembered Zion.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> We hanged our harps upon the willows in the midst thereof.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> For there they that carried us away captive required of us a song; and they that wasted us <em>required of us</em> mirth, <em>saying</em>, Sing us <em>one</em> of the songs of Zion.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> How shall we sing the LORD'S song in a strange land?

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> If I forget thee, O Jerusalem, let my right hand forget <em>her cunning</em>.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> If I do not remember thee, let my tongue cleave to the roof of my mouth; if I prefer not Jerusalem above my chief joy.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> Remember, O LORD, the children of Edom in the day of Jerusalem; who said, Rase <em>it</em>, rase <em>it, even</em> to the foundation thereof.

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> O daughter of Babylon, who art to be destroyed; happy <em>shall he be</em>, that rewardeth thee as thou hast served us.

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> Happy <em>shall he be</em>, that taketh and dasheth thy little ones against the stones.
