<sup class="verse-num">1</sup> O come, let us sing unto the LORD: let us make a joyful noise to the rock of our salvation.

<sup class="verse-num">2</sup> Let us come before his presence with thanksgiving, and make a joyful noise unto him with psalms.

<sup class="verse-num">3</sup> For the LORD <em>is</em> a great God, and a great King above all gods.

<sup class="verse-num">4</sup> In his hand <em>are</em> the deep places of the earth: the strength of the hills <em>is</em> his also.

<sup class="verse-num">5</sup> The sea <em>is</em> his, and he made it: and his hands formed the dry <em>land</em>.

<sup class="verse-num">6</sup> O come, let us worship and bow down: let us kneel before the LORD our maker.

<sup class="verse-num">7</sup> For he <em>is</em> our God; and we <em>are</em> the people of his pasture, and the sheep of his hand. To day if ye will hear his voice,

<sup class="verse-num">8</sup> Harden not your heart, as in the provocation, <em>and</em> as <em>in</em> the day of temptation in the wilderness:

<sup class="verse-num">9</sup> When your fathers tempted me, proved me, and saw my work.

<sup class="verse-num">10</sup> Forty years long was I grieved with <em>this</em> generation, and said, It <em>is</em> a people that do err in their heart, and they have not known my ways:

<sup class="verse-num">11</sup> Unto whom I sware in my wrath that they should not enter into my rest.
